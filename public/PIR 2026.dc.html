Decreasing / Fluctuating Enrollment Context
1 · Programs Implemented in Schools+
  • Early Registration Campaign
  • Balik-Eskwela Campaign
  • Oplan Balik-Eskwela Program
  • Learner Information System (LIS) updating and validation
  • School-based enrollment advocacy and orientation activities
  • Coordination with parents and barangay stakeholders during enrollment periods
2 · Limitations of These Programs+
  • Limited reach in remote areas resulting in delayed or missed enrollment information, misinformation, and lack of awareness among parents and learners
  • Limited counseling personnel and support services, compounded by financial difficulties of families and learner mobility due to relocation or employment needs
  • Low parent participation due to work schedules, language barriers, and limited community engagement structures
  • Limited budget allocation for sustained enrollment campaigns and outreach activities
  • Transportation and geographic barriers affecting access of learners in far-flung areas
3 · Programs That Should Be Strengthened+
  • Early Registration and Continuous Enrollment Advocacy Program
  • Oplan Balik-Eskwela Institutionalization and Expansion
  • Strengthened Community and Parent Engagement Program
  • Learner Tracking, Retention, and Re-enrollment Monitoring System
  • School-Community Partnership Strengthening Program (SGC + Barangay linkage)
4 · Programs That Should Be Implemented / Introduced+
  • JNIS Learner Outreach and Mapping Program (LOMP) – identifies learners in remote areas and tracks enrolment status annually
  • Community-Based Enrollment Advocacy Teams (CBEAT) – teachers, barangay partners, and parent leaders for a localized enrolment drive
  • Home-Based Learning Support and Counseling Program – direct support to struggling learners and at-risk dropouts
  • JHS to SHS Continuity Pathway Program – ensures smooth progression and reduces dropout during transition years
  • Integrated Literacy + Retention Intervention Program – links reading improvement directly with enrolment retention outcomes
5 · Why These Were Not Fully Addressed by the SDO+
  • The SDO operates within the approved budget and resources provided by higher levels of government, which may not be sufficient to fully support all schools' needs for enrollment campaigns, transportation, additional personnel, and community outreach.
  • Enrollment concerns are also affected by socio-economic and community factors such as poverty, migration, family responsibilities, and relocation - requiring stronger collaboration among the LGU, social welfare agencies, barangay partners, and other stakeholders beyond the SDO's direct mandate.
  • Limited manpower and competing priorities at the division level can affect continuous field monitoring, intensive child-finding activities, and sustained technical assistance to individual schools.
6 · Strengthening & Sustaining Programs+
JNIS Enrollment Sustainability and Partnership Program (JESP)
  • Establish stronger partnerships with LGU, barangay, parents, and community organizations
  • Develop a yearly enrollment sustainability plan
  • Mobilize stakeholders to support learner retention and school participation
JNIS Learner Assistance and Retention Support Program
  • Identification of learners needing support
  • Referral and linkage with appropriate agencies for assistance
  • Strengthening of school-based interventions for vulnerable learners

Strengthening Learning Outcomes

1 · Programs Implemented+
  • School-based structured reading and numeracy intervention sessions
  • Care for the NorthMin Reader – Education Summer Program (CNR-ESP) for Grades 4–12
  • Regional Remediation Program (RRP) for primary reading and mathematics
  • Wayground platform and tablets for data-driven quarterly testing and learner profiling
  • Teacher collaboration through School LAC sessions to share data metrics
2 · Limitations+
  • Teacher strain — faculty handle multiple grade levels and subjects, slowing intensive secondary-level tracking
  • Instructional gaps — beginning teachers (Teacher I and SHS faculty) still building confidence
  • Resource obsolescence — outdated pandemic-era materials; staggered MATATAG rollout (Grades 1 and 7)
  • Infrastructure bottlenecks — no dedicated ICT lab/library; spotty internet halts interactive software
3 · To Strengthen+
  • Data-driven intervention adjustments — deepen ELLNA, CRLA, NAT, and PHIL-IRI data to pinpoint micro-level gaps
  • Comprehensive faculty LAC sessions — Science of Reading (SoR), remediation, and numeracy strategies
  • Assessment literacy enhancement — standardize classroom tests for alignment, validity, and consistency
4 · To Implement / Introduce+
  • Secondary Filipino Literacy Pivot — JHS pipeline prioritizing Filipino reading mechanics and higher-order comprehension
  • Advanced Literacy Resource Sourcing — procure contemporary texts to reverse higher-level declines (e.g. ELLNA English)
  • Digital Integration Pathways — small-scale offline digital reading using the newly acquired SHS computer units
5 · Why Not Fully Addressed by the SDO+
  • Resource caps — the SDO operates within fixed government budgets, limiting funding for ICT labs or libraries
  • Division manpower constraints — limited personnel and heavy admin loads restrict localized mentoring and coaching
  • Socio-economic interference — student mobility (financial emergencies, relocation, seasonal employment) disrupts remediation
6 · Programs to Sustain+
  • Comprehensive Reading & Numeracy Quality Drive — secure SDO validation and TA on MS Teams, ARAL Tutors, and Reading Coach to preserve elementary growth
  • LGU-PTA Material Augmentation Linkage — leverage PTA and LGU to pool funding for modern media, updated resources, and learning facilities

Middle Performers Analysis

Performance Trend & Situation+

JNIS learner performance shows gradual improvement. NAT 6 results reflected positive gains across several learning areas, indicating interventions are contributing to learner improvement. Some learners still require continued support in literacy, numeracy, and mastery of competencies to reach higher proficiency.

Implemented Programs+
  • School-based reading and numeracy intervention programs
  • CNR-ESP (Care for the NorthMin Reader) for Grades 4–12
  • Regional Remediation Program (RRP) for reading and math difficulties
  • Remediation/enrichment activities based on assessment results
  • Learner progress monitoring
Key Execution Elements+
  • Assessment results used as the basis for planning interventions
  • Targeted support for struggling learners
  • Regular progress monitoring and stakeholder collaboration
  • Focus on core foundations
Institutional Best Practices+
  • Data-driven decision making using ELLNA, NAT, and classroom metrics
  • Early identification of learners needing intervention
  • Conduct of reading across grade levels
  • Differentiated instruction
  • Continuous teacher collaboration through LAC sessions
Division Technical Support+
  • Training on assessment administration and interpretation
  • Provision of Regional Quarterly Assessment materials and links
  • TA on literacy/numeracy programs and curriculum implementation
  • SDO coaching
Contributed Resources+
  • Human resources — K–SHS teachers executing the tracks
  • Learning resources — reading tools and assessment materials
  • Programs — CNR-ESP and RRP
  • Support systems — parent and community involvement
External Influences+
  • Support from the SDO via training
  • Availability of intervention frameworks
  • Parent participation in support activities
  • Stakeholder assistance in learning conditions
Core Challenges+
  • Some learners remain below expected proficiency despite interventions
  • Limited resources and facilities; no dedicated ICT laboratory or library
  • Multi-grade / multi-subject teaching distributions
  • Need for more intensive support pipelines
Requested Support+
  • Continuous training on Science of Reading (SoR), assessment strategies, and intervention approaches
  • Additional reading and numeracy materials
  • Strengthening of remediation programs
  • ICT equipment and digital learning resources
  • Continuous technical assistance from the SDO
